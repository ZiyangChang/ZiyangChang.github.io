Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of bio-inspired design, tactile sensing, and wearable devices. I am particularly motivated by how functional materials, intelligent algorithms, and bio-inspired principles can be integrated to endow robots with advanced perceptual abilities. The ultimate goal of my work is to develop multimodal sensing systems that empower both land-based and underwater robots to interact with, adapt to, and operate safely within complex, contact-rich, and hazardous environments where humans cannot easily go.

Whisker-inspired Sensor for Non-contact Underwater Perception
Ziyang Chang, Hao Li, Tianyu Tu, Miaoya Zhong, Mark Cutkosky
Undergraduate Visiting Researcher Program (UGVR)
poster

We present the design of whisker-inspired tactile sensors capable of measure flow velocity and sensing wake-induced vortexes.
Highly Transparent and Transferable Ultralong Carbon Nanotube Networks for Transparent Wearable Electronics
Fei Wang, Kangkang Wang, Ziyang Chang, Huarun Liang, Qinyuan Jiang, Aike Xi, Yanlong Zhao, Siming Zhao, Khaixien Leu, Xueke Wu, Run Li, Ya Huang, Yingying Zhang, Rufan Zhang
ACS Nano, 2024
paper

We developed highly transparent (>99%) and transferable ultralong carbon nanotube networks for transparent wearable electronics, achieving excellent electrical conductivity with minimal material usage through centimeter-scale continuous conductive pathways.
Review of controlled synthesis and performance regulation of functional carbon nanotube fibers
Run Li, Ziyang Chang, Rufan Zhang
Journal of Textile Research (IJRR)
paper

We review preparation methods and performance regulation strategies of CNTFs, summarizes recent advancements across multiple fields, and outlines future directions and challenges in this area.
